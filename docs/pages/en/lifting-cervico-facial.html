Facelift (Cervico-facial Lift)

What is a facelift?

The cervico-facial lift, commonly called facelift, is a surgical procedure that corrects signs of facial and neck aging. It allows lifting sagging tissues, smoothing wrinkles and restoring a youthful, rested appearance while preserving natural facial expressions.

What does a facelift treat?

A facelift can correct:

  • Sagging facial tissues (jowls)
  • Deep nasolabial folds
  • Marionette lines
  • Neck laxity and "turkey neck"
  • Double chin
  • Loss of jaw definition

Techniques

Several techniques exist adapted to the degree of aging:

  • Mini-lift: For early signs of aging, limited incisions, faster recovery
  • SMAS facelift: Classic technique lifting the muscular layer for lasting results
  • Deep plane facelift: More complete technique for significant aging

The facelift can be combined with eyelid surgery, lipofilling or neck liposuction.

The procedure

The procedure lasts 2 to 4 hours under general anesthesia. Hospitalization is 1 to 2 nights. Incisions are hidden around the ears and in the hairline.

Postoperative course

  • Dressing for 24 to 48 hours
  • Edema and bruising for 2 to 3 weeks
  • Sutures removed after 8 to 15 days
  • Social resumption after 2 to 3 weeks
  • Avoid sun exposure for 2 months

Final results are visible after 3 to 6 months.

Risks

Risks include hematoma, infection, wound healing problems, temporary facial nerve damage (very rare), asymmetry and visible scars. Dr. Woussen will inform you in detail.

Results

The facelift offers very natural results with a youthful, rested appearance. Results last 8 to 12 years on average. Aging continues but from a rejuvenated baseline.
