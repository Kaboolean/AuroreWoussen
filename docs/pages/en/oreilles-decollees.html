Protruding Ears (Otoplasty)

What is otoplasty?

Otoplasty is a surgical procedure that corrects protruding ears by repositioning them closer to the head and/or reshaping ear cartilage. This intervention can be performed on children from age 7 (when ears have reached adult size) as well as on adults.

Ear anomalies treated

Otoplasty can correct:

  • Protruding ears (excessively angled from the head)
  • Lack of antihelix fold (flat ear)
  • Overly large concha (central ear cavity)
  • Ear asymmetry
  • Overly large earlobes

Who is it for?

Otoplasty is indicated for:

  • Children from age 7 suffering from mockery or psychological discomfort
  • Adults embarrassed by their ear appearance
  • Anyone with protruding or asymmetrical ears

The procedure

The procedure lasts 1 to 1h30 and can be performed under local anesthesia (adults) or general anesthesia (children). It is usually done as an outpatient.

The technique involves reshaping cartilage through an incision behind the ear, leaving an invisible scar.

Postoperative course

  • Head bandage for 1 to 2 days
  • Headband to wear at night for 1 month
  • Edema and discomfort for a few days
  • No sports for 1 month
  • School or work resumption after a few days

Final results are visible immediately but take 2 to 3 months to fully settle.

Risks

Risks include hematoma, infection, asymmetry, over- or under-correction, abnormal scarring (keloid) and altered sensitivity. Dr. Woussen will inform you in detail.

Results

Otoplasty offers permanent results with naturally positioned, harmonious ears. This procedure has a significant positive impact on self-confidence, particularly in children. Patients can wear their hair short or pulled back with confidence.
