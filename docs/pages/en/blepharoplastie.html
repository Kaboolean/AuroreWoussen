Eyelid Surgery (Blepharoplasty)

What is blepharoplasty?

Blepharoplasty is a surgical procedure that corrects aging signs of the eyelids: excess skin, fat pockets (bags) and sagging. This intervention rejuvenates the eye area and provides a rested, open look.

Types of blepharoplasty

  • Upper blepharoplasty: Removes excess skin and possible fat pockets from upper eyelids. The scar is hidden in the natural eyelid crease.
  • Lower blepharoplasty: Treats bags under the eyes by removing or repositioning fat. The scar is hidden under the lashes or inside the eyelid (transconjunctival).
  • Four-eyelid blepharoplasty: Combines upper and lower blepharoplasty in a single procedure.

Who is it for?

Blepharoplasty is indicated for patients with:

  • Heavy, drooping upper eyelids
  • Bags under the eyes
  • Excess skin giving a tired look
  • Visual field obstruction by drooping upper eyelids

The procedure

Upper blepharoplasty lasts about 1 hour, lower about 1h30, four-eyelid about 2 hours. It can be performed under local or general anesthesia, usually as an outpatient.

Postoperative course

  • Cold compresses for 48 hours
  • Edema and bruising for 1 to 2 weeks
  • Sutures removed after 5 to 7 days
  • Avoid contact lenses for 2 weeks
  • Social resumption after 8 to 15 days

Scars are initially pink then become nearly invisible after 3 to 6 months.

Risks

Risks include hematoma, infection, dry eyes, asymmetry, incomplete eyelid closure (temporary) and unsatisfactory scarring. Dr. Woussen will inform you in detail.

Results

Blepharoplasty offers very natural results with an open, rested look. Results are long-lasting (10 to 15 years for upper eyelids). This procedure significantly improves the overall facial appearance.
