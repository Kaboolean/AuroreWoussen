Liposuction

What is liposuction?

Liposuction is a surgical procedure that permanently removes localized fat deposits resistant to diet and exercise. This technique aspirates fat cells (adipocytes) using fine cannulas, allowing harmonious reshaping of the silhouette.

It is important to understand that liposuction is not a weight loss method but a body contouring technique intended to treat localized fat deposits in people with stable weight.

Treatable areas

Liposuction can treat many body areas:

  • Abdomen: Belly, flanks (love handles)
  • Hips: Saddlebags
  • Thighs: Inner and outer surface
  • Knees: Inner surface
  • Arms: Inner surface
  • Back: Bra line bulges
  • Face and neck: Double chin, jowls
  • Calves and ankles: In case of localized excess

Multiple areas can be treated during the same procedure.

Preoperative consultation

During the consultation, Dr. Woussen will evaluate your skin quality, location and extent of fat deposits. She will determine if you are a good candidate for liposuction and explain the realistic results you can expect.

A complete clinical examination will be performed, as well as preoperative photos. Dr. Woussen will explain the technique, postoperative course, risks and answer all your questions. A detailed quote will be provided.

Liposuction is more effective in people with good skin elasticity, allowing the skin to retract after fat aspiration.

The procedure

The procedure lasts 1 to 3 hours depending on the number of areas treated and volume to be aspirated. It can be performed under local, regional or general anesthesia depending on the extent of treatment.

The technique includes several stages:

  • Infiltration: A solution containing local anesthetic and vasoconstrictor is injected into areas to be treated to facilitate aspiration and reduce bleeding.
  • Aspiration: Fine cannulas (2 to 4 mm diameter) are introduced through small incisions of a few millimeters. Fat is aspirated evenly and homogeneously through back-and-forth movements.
  • Closure: Incisions are closed with stitches or left open to promote drainage.

Liposuction can be performed as an outpatient or with an overnight stay depending on the volume aspirated.

Postoperative course

The postoperative course is marked by:

  • Significant edema (swelling) for 2 to 4 weeks
  • Bruising for 2 to 3 weeks
  • Moderate pain, comparable to muscle soreness
  • Pink fluid drainage from incisions for a few days

A compression garment (girdle, shorts) must be worn day and night for 4 to 6 weeks to promote skin retraction and limit edema.

Daily activities can be resumed after a few days. Sports should be avoided for 4 to 6 weeks. Final results are visible after 3 to 6 months, once the edema has completely resolved.

Risks and complications

Like any surgical procedure, liposuction carries risks:

  • Hematoma or infection (rare)
  • Surface irregularities (bumps, hollows)
  • Asymmetry
  • Temporary skin hyperpigmentation
  • Altered sensation (usually temporary)
  • Skin laxity if skin lacks elasticity
  • Fat embolism (exceptional)

Dr. Woussen will inform you in detail of all risks during the preoperative consultation.

Results

Liposuction offers permanent results because removed fat cells do not regenerate. The silhouette is slimmed and harmoniously reshaped. To maintain results, it is essential to maintain a healthy lifestyle (balanced diet, regular physical activity).

In case of significant weight gain, fat may deposit in other untreated areas.
