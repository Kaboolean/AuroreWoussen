Gynecomastia

What is gynecomastia?

Gynecomastia is an excessive development of the mammary gland in men, giving a more or less marked "breast" appearance. This condition can be unilateral (one side) or bilateral (both sides) and can be a source of significant aesthetic and psychological discomfort.

It is important to distinguish true gynecomastia (excess mammary gland) from adipomastia (excess fat without glandular development), as treatment differs. In many cases, both components are associated.

Causes of gynecomastia

Gynecomastia can have several origins:

  • Physiological: Common at certain periods of life (newborn, puberty, elderly man) due to natural hormonal variations
  • Hormonal: Imbalance between estrogen and testosterone
  • Medication-related: Certain medications can promote gynecomastia (anti-androgens, certain antidepressants, etc.)
  • Pathological: Certain diseases can be the cause (testicular tumors, liver failure, hyperthyroidism, etc.)
  • Idiopathic: In many cases, no cause is found

A medical workup is performed before any surgery to rule out a pathological cause requiring specific treatment.

Types of gynecomastia

Several types are distinguished according to the composition of excess tissues:

  • Glandular gynecomastia: Predominance of firm glandular tissue. Treatment by surgical excision of the gland.
  • Adipomastia: Predominance of fatty tissue. Treatment by liposuction.
  • Mixed gynecomastia: Combination of gland and fat. Treatment combining liposuction and glandular excision.

Preoperative consultation

During the consultation, Dr. Woussen will perform a clinical examination to determine the type of gynecomastia and its extent. She will look for a possible cause and may request additional tests (hormonal workup, breast ultrasound).

She will explain the surgical technique adapted to your case, expected results, scars, risks and postoperative course. Preoperative photos will be taken and a detailed quote will be provided.

The procedure

The procedure lasts approximately 1 to 2 hours and is performed under general anesthesia, usually as an outpatient.

Depending on the type of gynecomastia, the procedure may include:

  • Liposuction: To remove excess fat. Small incisions of a few millimeters are made.
  • Glandular excision: To remove the mammary gland. A semi-circular incision at the lower edge of the areola allows removal of glandular tissue.
  • Skin resection: In case of significant excess skin, skin resection may be necessary.

A compressive dressing and compression garment are applied at the end of the procedure.

Postoperative course

The postoperative course is generally straightforward:

  • Edema and bruising for 2 to 3 weeks
  • Moderate pain, well controlled by painkillers
  • Wearing a compression garment for 4 to 6 weeks
  • Resumption of daily activities after a few days
  • No sports for 4 to 6 weeks

Scars are discreet: at the areola edge for glandular excision, a few millimeters for liposuction. They gradually fade over several months.

The final result is visible after 3 to 6 months, once the edema has completely resolved.

Risks and complications

Like any surgical procedure, gynecomastia treatment carries risks:

  • Hematoma or infection (rare)
  • Residual asymmetry
  • Surface irregularities
  • Altered areola sensation (usually temporary)
  • Insufficient or excessive resection
  • Visible scars

Dr. Woussen will inform you in detail of all risks during the consultation.

Results

Surgical treatment of gynecomastia offers excellent results with a flat, masculine chest. This procedure allows regaining a normal silhouette and feeling comfortable shirtless. The positive psychological impact is often considerable, with a notable improvement in self-confidence.
